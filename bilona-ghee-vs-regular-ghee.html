Understanding the Difference

Most people use the terms "bilona ghee" and "regular ghee" interchangeably, but they're fundamentally different products with vastly different nutritional values. Understanding these differences is crucial when choosing ghee for your family's health.

What is Bilona Ghee?

Bilona ghee is made using the ancient, traditional churning method that's been used in India for thousands of years. The word "bilona" literally means "to churn." In this method, whole milk is first fermented into yogurt, then churned using wooden tools to extract butter. This butter is then gently heated over a slow flame to produce pure ghee. The entire process is manual and labor-intensive but preserves all beneficial compounds.

Why Bilona Matters: This slow, careful process doesn't damage heat-sensitive nutrients and beneficial enzymes, making bilona ghee superior in nutritional value.

What is Regular Ghee?

Regular or industrial ghee is produced using modern commercial methods. Cream is separated from milk using high-speed centrifugation, then heated to high temperatures (often above 100°C) in large industrial vats. This process is faster and more profitable but destroys many beneficial compounds. Many brands also use additives, preservatives, or mix in cheaper oils to maximize profit margins.
