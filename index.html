    Absolutely. Gaumaatri ghee is made purely from the milk of indigenous Badri cows using the traditional Vedic Bilona method. Every batch is lab tested for purity and is free from any additives or preservatives.

    The Bilona method is an ancient Vedic process where cow milk is first boiled, cooled, and set into curd. The curd is then hand-churned using a wooden churner to extract butter (makhan), which is then slow-cooked on low flame to produce pure ghee. This process preserves all nutrients and gives ghee its distinctive grainy texture.

    When stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, our ghee stays fresh for up to 12 months. There's no need to refrigerate — in fact, ghee keeps better at room temperature. Always use a clean, dry spoon to scoop ghee to maintain freshness.
